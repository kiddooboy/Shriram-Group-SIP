1. The Big Picture — What the AI Does

The Shriram Group SIP platform uses an eight-model AI engine to answer one question for every employee: "Given who you are, what you earn, what you want, and how the market looks right now — which Shriram AMC fund(s) should you invest in, for how much, and for how long?"

It does this not through a one-size-fits-all rule like "aggressive = small-cap" but through a real-time, personalized pipeline that ingests your profile, your goals, live market data, and the behavioral patterns of thousands of employees like you — then optimises the answer to maximise the probability that you actually reach your goal.

The core promise: The AI recommends the fund, the exact SIP amount, and the tenure most likely to get you to your specific goal — and it tells you why, in your language.

2. Step 1 · Know You: The Adaptive Questionnaire

Before the AI can recommend anything, it needs to understand your financial life. Instead of a static 20-question survey that most people rush through, the engine uses an adaptive Bayesian questionnaire — a smart interview that asks only the questions most useful for you specifically, based on what it already knows.

How it works

When you sign in, the system already knows your age (from Aadhaar KYC), your employer entity, designation, and location (from SSO). It immediately builds a starting-point estimate of your likely financial profile by comparing you to thousands of employees in the same age-band, designation tier, and city type from industry data.

Then the questionnaire begins. After each answer, the AI recalculates which remaining question would teach it the most about you. Questions that would be redundant (because your answers already make them obvious) are skipped. This is why most employees only see 9–11 of the 13 questions.

Bayesian adaptive engine: The system maintains a probability distribution over your risk capacity, savings ability, time horizon, and behavioral biases. Each answer narrows that distribution. When it is narrow enough — confident enough — it stops asking and starts recommending.

3. Step 2 · Understand the Market: Fund & Regime Analysis

While the questionnaire is running, the engine is simultaneously analysing the mutual fund landscape — not to pick stocks, but to understand which category of fund, at what risk level, is most appropriate given current market conditions.

Fund Universe Ingestion

Every night the system ingests data on all ~2,000 mutual fund schemes across all 44 AMCs in India — NAV history, holdings, risk metrics, manager tenure, and factsheet disclosures. This full-universe data is used exclusively for benchmarking: understanding where each Shriram AMC fund sits relative to its peers.

Important: The AI only recommends Shriram AMC's own schemes. The full universe is ingested so the system can honestly tell you "this fund is in the top quartile of its category for 3-year risk-adjusted returns" — a claim that requires seeing all 44 AMCs.

Market Regime Detection (Model M4)

The engine classifies the current market regime (risk-on, neutral, risk-off, or high-volatility) every day using macro and market indicators: index volatility, market breadth, yield curve shape, FII flows, and valuation percentiles. This does not trigger market timing for your SIP — SIPs work best with regular investing regardless of conditions. Instead, the regime:

  • Conditions the return-forecast models (more conservative in high-vol regimes)
  • Adjusts messaging — e.g., surfacing the "SIPs buy more units when markets fall" nudge during volatile periods
  • Tilts new-allocation defaults slightly more conservative in risk-off conditions

Return Forecasting (Model M5)

Rather than a single projected return number, the AI builds a distribution: optimistic, median, and pessimistic scenarios per fund, per goal, conditioned on the current regime. These are what power the "probability of reaching your goal" figure shown in the recommendation. Forecasts are deliberately shrunk toward long-run category averages — the system is cautious, not bullish.

4. Step 3 · Match & Optimise: The Recommendation Engine

This is where your profile meets the fund universe. The engine does not simply look up a rule table ("moderate risk → balanced fund"). It uses machine learning to find the best fit and then runs an optimiser to determine the exact amount and tenure.

1

User Embedding (Model M1)

Your questionnaire answers, demographic data, and behavioral signals are compressed into a 64-dimensional mathematical representation of you — your financial personality, as the AI understands it. The six employee archetypes (Starter, Grower, Consolidator, Accelerator, Pre-Retiree, Near-Exit) are cluster priors in this space — your profile gravitates toward the most similar archetype but is never hard-bucketed into one.

2

Fund Embedding (Model M2)

Each Shriram AMC fund is represented as a 64-dimensional vector in the same space as your profile, based on its risk profile, return characteristics, category, holdings, and cost. This shared space means we can directly measure how well a fund "fits" you mathematically.

3

Two-Tower Matching (Model M3)

The engine computes a fit score between your profile vector and each Shriram AMC fund vector. Funds are ranked by suitability. This ranking is trained on real outcome data: pairings that led to goal attainment and SIP persistence score higher than those that led to early redemptions or bounces.

4

RL Portfolio Optimiser (Model M6)

Given the shortlisted funds and the distributional return forecasts, a Reinforcement Learning optimiser determines the exact SIP amount and tenure for each of your goals. Its objective function is not "maximise expected return" but "maximise the probability you reach your goal amount by your target date" — while respecting your stress-test SIP floor, your liquidity constraint, your no-go preferences, and your risk capacity. The output is the recommendation: fund, ₹ amount, tenure, and a goal-attainment probability.

Why RL? Traditional optimisers maximise expected value, which means they can recommend a high-risk path that, on average, looks good — but has a meaningful chance of missing your goal. The RL approach directly maximises P(goal met), making it more conservative and goal-aligned.

What if the engine can't serve a goal?

If your goal requires a product that Shriram AMC does not offer — for example, an ELSS fund for 80C tax-saving (Shriram AMC has no ELSS scheme), or an international fund — the engine explicitly flags this rather than forcing a poor-fit recommendation. You will see an honest message: "For 80C tax-saving, consider an ELSS scheme from another AMC — this platform does not offer one."

5. Step 4 · Explain & Communicate: Plain-Language Rationale

A recommendation is only useful if the employee understands it and trusts it. The engine therefore generates a two-layer explanation for every recommendation.

Layer 1 — SHAP Attribution (internal)

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) is a technique from explainable AI that identifies exactly which inputs drove the recommendation and by how much. For example: "6-year goal horizon contributed +18% to tenure, moderate crash-scenario answer contributed −12% to equity allocation, ₹4,500 stress-floor contributed −8% to SIP amount."

This layer is stored for compliance and audit purposes, and feeds Layer 2.

Layer 2 — LLM Plain-Language Rationale (employee-facing)

The top SHAP factors are passed to a language model that generates 3–4 plain sentences in the employee's chosen language (Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Marathi, Bengali, Malayalam, or English). Crucially, the language model is constrained: it may only reference factors that appear in the SHAP output and facts about the specific fund — it cannot invent claims, exaggerate returns, or mention funds not in the recommendation.

Example rationale (English):
"Based on your 6-year goal of ₹10 lakh for your child's education and your preference to pause SIPs only when income drops significantly, we recommend the Shriram Multi Asset Allocation Fund. A monthly SIP of ₹6,200 gives you an 82% probability of reaching ₹10 lakh by 2031. This fund sits in the top quartile of its category for 3-year risk-adjusted return."

Mandatory disclosures (always shown)

  • Related-party disclosure: "Shriram Finance distributes Shriram AMC funds. Both are part of Shriram Group."
  • Market risk: "Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks. Read all scheme-related documents carefully."
  • Projections show a range (optimistic / median / pessimistic), never a single guaranteed number.

6. The Eight AI Models — At a Glance

M1

User Embedding

Converts your complete profile into a 64-dimensional vector representing your financial personality. Robust to missing answers — degrades gracefully when questionnaire is incomplete.

M2

Fund Embedding

Encodes every mutual fund (all 2,000 schemes) in the same 64-dimensional space as M1, enabling direct profile–fund similarity measurement. Shriram AMC funds are tagged recommendable; all others are benchmark-only.

M3

Two-Tower Matcher

Computes user–fund fit scores across all Shriram AMC schemes. Trained on real outcome data — pairings that led to goal attainment score highest. Produces the ranked shortlist.

M4

Market Regime Detector

Classifies current market conditions (risk-on / neutral / risk-off / high-vol) daily from macro indicators. Conditions return forecasts and messaging — never triggers market timing.

M5

Return Forecast Ensemble

Produces distributional (p10 / p50 / p90) forward return estimates per fund, conditioned on the regime. Conservatively shrunk toward long-run category averages.

M6

RL Portfolio Optimiser

The decision engine. Determines SIP amount and tenure to maximise goal-attainment probability under your risk capacity, liquidity, and stress-floor constraints.

M7

Contextual-Bandit Nudge

Selects the best behavioral prompt for you (step-up reminder, dip reassurance, protection-gap alert). Optimises for healthy financial action, not raw app engagement. Frequency-capped.

M8

Adaptive Questionnaire

Bayesian belief-update engine that selects the next most-informative question to ask. Stops early (at 9–11 questions) once profile confidence is sufficient. Resumable across sessions.

7. Why Only Shriram AMC Funds?

The platform operates as a captive Mutual Fund Distributor (MFD) — similar to how SBI Mutual Fund is sold through SBI branches. Shriram Finance holds an AMFI ARN and distributes only Shriram AMC's schemes. This is a deliberate business and regulatory choice.

8. Cold Start — What Happens on Day 1

The system must work well even before it has seen a single real outcome. Four mechanisms ensure the first recommendation is still useful and conservative:

  1. Industry cohort priors: Starting estimates built from AMFI investor demographics, SEBI/RBI household-finance surveys, and published persona research, segmented by age, designation tier, location, and entity type. A new employee starts at their cohort's prior.
  2. Cluster-similarity weighting: For the first 90 days, your recommendation is a blend of the personal model (initially weak) and the cohort cluster recommendation (stronger). As your profile fills in and behavioral data accumulates, the personal model's weight rises from 20% to 90%.
  3. Conservative defaults: Until the profile-confidence score passes its threshold, the engine defaults to the Shriram Multi Asset Allocation Fund, uses the stress-tested SIP floor (not the optimistic figure), and assumes a shorter horizon. You can never be worse off for the system being unsure about you.
  4. Synthetic bootstrapping: ~50,000 synthetic employee profiles — sampled from the Shriram workforce distribution — are used to pre-train M1/M2/M3/M6 before real data exists, so models are not starting from zero.

9. Behavioral Intelligence

The questionnaire captures your stated preferences. Behavioral signals capture your revealed preferences — what you actually do under pressure. These signals flow into the user embedding from day one:

Privacy: Behavioral data is disclosed in the onboarding consent flow, used only for product personalisation, and is never visible to your employer or HR. It is deleted one year after you leave the Group (DPDP Act compliance).

Fairness audits

Before any new model version is deployed, it must pass a fairness check: recommendations and nudges are tested for disparate impact across age, gender, location tier, and entity. The measure is comparable goal-attainment probability across groups — not identical recommendations, but equitable outcomes. A failure blocks deployment.

Model governance

  • Champion/challenger: New model versions run in shadow (no employee impact) before promotion; promoted only after offline metrics + fairness pass.
  • Drift monitoring: Input drift, prediction drift, and outcome drift are alarmed; automatic rollback on breach.
  • Human oversight: An Investment Committee + Model Risk reviewer sign off all model promotions and review a sample of recommendations each quarter.
  • Immutable audit log: Every recommendation is stored with its full input vector, model versions, SHAP values, and rationale text in a write-once log retained for 8 years. Any recommendation can be fully reconstructed.
